- **Bouncer's in.** ✅ Let me decode those two lines, they're the whole concept:

  ```python
  @ratelimit(key='user', rate='3/h', block=True)   # OTP send
  @ratelimit(key='ip',   rate='5/m', block=True)   # refresh
  ```

  Three settings, that's it:

  | Setting | Means |
  |---|---|
  | `key='user'` | count per **logged-in user** |
  | `key='ip'` | count per **visitor's IP address** |
  | `rate='3/h'` | **3 times per hour** (5/m = 5 per minute) |
  | `block=True` | when they go over → **block them automatically** |

**Why two different keys?**
- `send_otp` requires login → we know *who* they are → limit per **user**
- `trigger_fetch` has no login → we only know their **IP** → limit per **IP**
