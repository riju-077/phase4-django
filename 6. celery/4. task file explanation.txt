@shared_task

@shared_task is the sticker that says "this function is a Celery job — a recipe the cook can make." Without it, it's just a normal function. With it, you gain the magic .delay() power (clip it to the rail).

fetch_weather(city, latitude, longitude) — the job takes three inputs: which city, and its map coordinates. So you can reuse the same recipe for London, Tokyo, anywhere.

Chunk 2 — phone the weather service

url = (f"https://api.open-meteo.com/v1/forecast"
       f"?latitude={latitude}&longitude={longitude}"
       f"&hourly=temperature_2m&forecast_days=1")
response = httpx.get(url, timeout=10)
response.raise_for_status()
data = response.json()

- url = the web address of the free weather service, with your coordinates plugged in. It's asking: "give me hourly temperature for 1 day at this spot."
- httpx.get(url) = actually phone that address over the internet and get the answer back. (timeout=10 = "give up if it takes more than 10 seconds.")
- raise_for_status() = "if the weather service returned an error, stop right here and complain." A safety check.
- data = response.json() = the service replies in JSON (text data); this turns it into a Python object you can read.
