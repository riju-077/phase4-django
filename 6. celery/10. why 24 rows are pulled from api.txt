 Why 24 rows — not 16 or 30?

Because you asked the weather API for exactly that. Look at the URL your task builds:
...&hourly=temperature_2m&forecast_days=1
Two words control it:
- hourly = "give me one value per hour"
- forecast_days=1 = "for 1 day"

1 day × 24 hours = 24 values → 24 rows. The number isn't random; it's math from your request.

Want proof it's under your control? Change the request:

┌─────────────────────────┬─────────────────────┐
│       You ask for       │    Rows you get     │
├─────────────────────────┼─────────────────────┤
│ forecast_days=1         │ 24 (1 day, hourly)  │
├─────────────────────────┼─────────────────────┤
│ forecast_days=2         │ 48 (2 days, hourly) │
├─────────────────────────┼─────────────────────┤
│ daily instead of hourly │ ~1 per day          │
└─────────────────────────┴─────────────────────┘

So "24" is just "hourly readings for 1 day." If you wanted 16 or 30, you'd change the API parameters — the database rows are a direct mirror of what you requested from Open-Meteo.
