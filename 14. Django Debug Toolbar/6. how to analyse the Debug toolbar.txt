Good question — and yes, your page is healthy. Here's how to judge it, simply.

Two things to watch:

1. Number of queries (the more important one)
- A few (like your 2) → ✅ healthy
- Dozens (30, 50+) → 🚩 something's wrong, go investigate

2. Total time
- Under ~100ms → snappy
- A few hundred ms → fine
- Over ~1 second → 🚩 users start to feel it

---
About your 168ms — don't worry about it. That's just the first database connection warming up (the very first query of the session pays a one-time cost). Reload the page and you'll see it drop. The 5.2ms is the "true" speed.

The real health signal on your page is the query COUNT: just 2. That's the green light. 🟢

---
Simple rule to remember:

▎ Glance at the query count first. Single digits = relax. Suddenly dozens = you broke something, go look.

The toolbar's job is to make that number impossible to miss. That's it.
