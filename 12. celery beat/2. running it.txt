That's a Windows quirk — it won't let you combine them. No problem, and honestly it pushes you toward the production pattern anyway: worker and beat run as two separate services.

So you need two celery terminals now (plus runserver = 3 total):

Terminal A — the worker (the delivery guy):
celery -A dashboard worker --loglevel=info --pool=solo

Terminal B — beat, the alarm clock (its own window):
celery -A dashboard beat --loglevel=info

How they team up:
- Beat (Terminal B) rings every 2 min → drops the job in Redis. It only schedules, never does the work.
- Worker (Terminal A) picks the job out of Redis → actually fetches the weather.

So watch both windows:
- Terminal B: Scheduler: Sending due task fetch-london-weather
- Terminal A: Task weather.tasks.fetch_weather... succeeded

Fire up both and tell me what you see. 👀

⚠️ One thing before you walk away: that bot fetches London every 2 minutes right now. If you leave all three terminals running, it'll keep hitting the API and filling your DB. When you're done testing, just Ctrl+C the beat terminal (Terminal B) to stop the clock — or swap to minute=0 for hourly.
